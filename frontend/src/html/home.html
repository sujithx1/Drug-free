Understanding Drug Risks

Opioids

Powerful painkillers that can lead to severe addiction and respiratory depression.

  • Examples: Heroin, Fentanyl, Oxycodone, Hydrocodone
  • Short-term effects: Pain relief, euphoria, drowsiness, nausea
  • Long-term effects: Severe physical dependence, compromised immune system, risk of overdose
  • Withdrawal symptoms: Muscle pain, insomnia, diarrhea, vomiting, cold flashes
Learn More

Stimulants

Drugs that increase alertness, attention, and energy while raising blood pressure and heart rate.

  • Examples: Cocaine, Methamphetamine, MDMA, Adderall when abused
  • Short-term effects: Increased energy, reduced appetite, elevated blood pressure
  • Long-term effects: Cardiovascular damage, malnutrition, psychosis, severe dental problems
  • Withdrawal symptoms: Depression, fatigue, increased appetite, disturbed sleep patterns
Learn More

Depressants

Substances that decrease central nervous system activity, often leading to sedation.

  • Examples: Alcohol, Benzodiazepines, Barbiturates, GHB
  • Short-term effects: Relaxation, drowsiness, reduced inhibition, impaired coordination
  • Long-term effects: Liver damage, memory problems, sleep disorders, increased risk of accidents
  • Withdrawal symptoms: Anxiety, insomnia, tremors, seizures (can be life-threatening)
